The Best Movies accroding to Angela

My top 3 movies of all-time

Paterson (2016)

One of my favorite movies is Paterson (2016), directed by Jim Jarmusch. It’s about a bus driver named Paterson who lives in the city of Paterson, New Jersey. He writes poetry in his spare time, and the film shows his daily life, focusing on the small, beautiful moments he experiences. What I love about the movie is how it finds meaning in the ordinary. Paterson’s life isn’t dramatic, but the film highlights the beauty in simple things like his conversations with people or his walks around the city. It’s a very calm, almost meditative movie that makes you reflect on your own life. Overall, Paterson is a beautiful film that shows how we can find poetry in everyday life.

A One and a Two (2000)

A movie I really like is A One and a Two (2000), directed by Edward Yang. It’s a Taiwanese film about a family going through personal struggles. The movie is quiet but powerful, focusing on the emotions and relationships of the characters. I love how it shows the complexity of family life, and how everyone deals with challenges in their own way. The cinematography is beautiful, with long shots that make the movie feel calm and reflective. Overall, it’s a film that stays with you because of its emotional depth and realistic portrayal of life.

The Godfather (1972)

A movie I really like is The Godfather (1972). It’s about a Mafia family and their struggles with power, loyalty, and family. The main character, Vito Corleone, tries to protect his family while dealing with crime and betrayal. I love how the film shows the conflict between family values and the violent world they live in. The acting, especially by Marlon Brando, is outstanding, and the film has many iconic scenes that are unforgettable. Overall, The Godfather is a masterpiece because of its deep story, great performances, and timeless appeal.
